My Favourite Vanilla Cupcakes Recipe

Posted on March 2022 / author: Richard Taylor

Order now!

Vanilla Cupcakes are some of the most loved sweets by our generation. The reason is simple, they are super easy to make and the texture is light, fluffy, and melt-in-your-mouth good. Vanilla cupcakes might have frostings or glazes, or they can remain plain and uncovered. The name of these cupcakes is based on the flavor of the cupcakes and not the frosting, so it's not unusual to see vanilla cupcakes that have chocolate or caramel frostings, or even other flavors.

Vanilla cupcakes usually use the same recipe as plain vanilla cake but with a shorter cooking time. The smaller size of the cupcakes means that there is more surface area on them than on a single full-size cake, and the heat from the oven won’t need as much time to cook the center of the cupcake. Some vanilla cupcakes are made using other ingredients to accommodate people who have allergies. These cupcakes must use vanilla, of course, but some recipes omit eggs or milk to make the cupcakes edible to people who can’t have those ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour.
  • 1 1/4 tsp baking powder.
  • 1/2 tsp salt.
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened.
  • 3/4 cup sugar.
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature.
  • 2 tsp pure vanilla extract.
  • 1/2 cup buttermilk, or plain kefir, room temperature.

NOTE:Some people have reported having trouble with this recipe, while others love it. There could be any number of factors such as: improper measuring of ingredients, oven type, altitude, etc. If you’d prefer a more traditional vanilla cupcake, the suggestion would beto use butter, a little bit of oil, as well as a different creaming method, which seems to be less finicky for people.


Tips for the best Vanilla Cake Receipe

Room Temperature Ingredients
Eggs, buttermilk and butter must be at room temperature to keep your batter an even consistency.

Alternate Adding Flour and Buttermilk
Doing so, keeps the mixture uniform and well blended. If you add in all of the flour or buttermilk at once, it can saturate the creamed butter and cause the mixture to separate. Mix well between each addition.

Measure Correctly
Be sure to use the proper measuring cups for wet and dry ingredients. Watch our video on measuring ingredients to ensure great results every time.

Don’t Overfill
Fill cupcake liners 2/3 full or they will overflow and cause a muffin top. If you divide evenly between 12 cupcakes, you should have exactly enough batter.
