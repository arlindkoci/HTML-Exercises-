Tiramisu is a classic Italian no-bake dessert made with layers of coffee-soaked ladyfingers and incredible mascarpone cream.

The word Tiramisu (tirami su in Italian) means “cheer me up” or “pull me up,” which is very appropriate because one bite will put you in a good mood! An authentic Tiramisu dessert that has 2 main layers: sponge cake ladyfingers soaked in coffee and liqueur and the mascarpone cream layer. This classic dessert often contains raw egg yolks, but we use a different process to create a custard-like cream with amazing texture and flavor.

INGREDIENTS

  • 4 large egg yolks
  • ½ cup/100 grams granulated sugar, divided
  • ¾ cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup/227 grams mascarpone (8 ounces)
  • 1¾ cups good espresso or very strong coffee
  • 2 tablespoons rum or cognac
  • 2 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder
  • About 24 ladyfingers (from one 7-ounce/200-gram package)
  • 1 to 2 ounces bittersweet chocolate, for shaving (optional)

The above recipe has a yield of 6 to 8 servings. To make this recipe even more delicious we can add Mascarpone which is essentially the Italian version of cream cheese. It is made of whipped cream, stabilized with an acidic ingredient such as lemon juice or citric acid. Mascarpone cheese has a smoother consistency, is milder in flavor and has twice as much fat as cream cheese.

Tips for the Best Tiramisu:

Quick Dip
Dip ladyfingers one or two at a time. A quick dip on each side is enough. There will be some syrup left behind.
Water Bath
When whisking yolks and sugar over steam, make sure the bowl is not touching the water beneath it.
Do not over-mix
When folding cream into Mascarpone, do not overmix the cream or it may turn grainy.
Do not over-soak the ladyfingers
Do not over-soak the ladyfingers or the layers may collaps.
Chill the cake
You can keep the cake at least 6 hours in the refrigerator or overnight and it will be very easy to slice.
